Midwife Companion

NHS

Analgesia

Paracetamol PO IV
Indication: Mild-moderate pain, pyrexia.
Dose: 1g every 4-6 hours (Max 4g in 24h)
Reduce dose if weight <50kg or hepatic impairment.
Dihydrocodeine PO
Indication: Moderate postnatal pain.
Dose: 30mg every 4-6 hours
Caution in breastfeeding (monitor baby for drowsiness). Avoid if baby premature/apnoeic.
Pethidine IM
Indication: 1st stage labour analgesia.
Dose: 50-100mg IM
Crosses placenta. Can cause neonatal resp depression and poor feeding. Have Naloxone available.
Diamorphine IM
Indication: Strong labour analgesia.
Dose: 5-10mg IM
Controlled Drug. Stronger euphoria/sedation than pethidine. Anti-emetic usually required.
Entonox INH
Indication: Labour pain (Gas & Air).
Dose: 50:50 O2/N2O via mouthpiece
Notes: Rapid onset/offset. Can cause dry mouth, nausea, lightheadedness.
Lidocaine 1% SC
Indication: Perineal infiltration (Suturing/Episiotomy).
Max: 20ml of 1% (200mg) usually safe limit
Always aspirate before injecting to avoid intravascular injection.

Uterotonics / PPH

Syntometrine IM
Indication: Active management of 3rd stage.
Dose: 1 ampoule (1ml) IM
⛔ CONTRAINDICATED in Hypertension / Pre-eclampsia (Contains Ergometrine).
Syntocinon (Oxytocin) IM IV
Indication: 3rd Stage or IOL.
3rd Stage: 10 IU IM or 5 IU IV (Slow)
IOL: IV Infusion (Variable rate)
Ergometrine IM IV
Indication: Treatment of PPH (atonic uterus).
Dose: 500mcg IM or 250mcg IV (Slow)
Causes strong, sustained contraction. Nausea/Vomiting common. C/I in Hypertension.
Carboprost IM
Indication: Severe PPH refractory to other agents.
Dose: 250mcg Deep IM. Repeat q15min (Max 8 doses)
⛔ C/I in Asthma (Causes bronchospasm). Side effect: Severe Diarrhoea.
Misoprostol PR PO
Indication: PPH treatment or IOL.
PPH: 800-1000mcg Rectally (PR) or Sublingual
IOL: 25mcg Vaginal/Oral (Low dose!)

Emergency / Hypertension

Labetalol PO IV
Indication: Hypertension / Pre-eclampsia.
PO: 200mg (Adjust as per protocol)
⛔ C/I in Asthma. Monitor BP/Pulse.
Nifedipine (MR) PO
Indication: Hypertension (Second line or if Asthmatic).
Dose: 10mg - 20mg Modified Release
Never give Sublingually (Risk of stroke from rapid BP drop).
Magnesium Sulphate IV
Indication: Prevent/Treat Eclampsia & Neuroprotection (Pre-term).
Loading: 4g IV over 5-15 mins
Monitor: Resp Rate (<12), Reflexes (Loss), Urine Output (<30ml/hr). Antidote: Calcium Gluconate.
Calcium Gluconate IV
Indication: Magnesium Toxicity Antidote.
Dose: 1g (10ml of 10%) IV over 10 mins
Adrenaline 1:1000 IM
Indication: Anaphylaxis.
Dose: 500mcg (0.5ml) IM Anterolateral thigh
Repeat every 5 mins if no improvement. Call 2222.

Anti-Emetics

Cyclizine IM IV
Indication: Nausea/Vomiting (Opioid induced).
Dose: 50mg IM or slow IV
Painful on IV injection. Can cause drowsiness.
Ondansetron IM IV
Indication: Hyperemesis / Post-op nausea.
Dose: 4mg IM or slow IV
Avoid in 1st Trimester if possible (small cleft risk). Non-sedating.
Metoclopramide IM IV
Indication: Nausea / Lactation stimulation.
Dose: 10mg IM/IV
Risk of Oculogyric Crisis (esp. young women). Avoid <20 years age.

Antibiotics / Others

Benzylpenicillin IV
Indication: GBS Prophylaxis in labour.
Load: 3g IV. Maint: 1.5g IV q4h until birth
Check Allergy status! If Penicillin allergic, usually Clindamycin or Vancomycin.
Gentamicin IV
Indication: Sepsis (Red Flag) / Chorioamnionitis.
Dose: 5mg/kg (Check local trust weight tables)
Ototoxic/Nephrotoxic. Usually single dose in labour.
Metronidazole IV PO
Indication: Anaerobic infections / Sepsis / Trichomoniasis.
Dose: 500mg IV TDS or 400mg PO TDS
Daltheparin/Enoxaparin SC
Indication: VTE Prophylaxis (LMWH).
Dose: Weight dependent (e.g. 5000 units / 40mg)
Avoid epidural/spinal for 12h after dose (prophylactic) or 24h (treatment).
Iron (Ferrous Sulphate) PO
Indication: Iron Deficiency Anaemia.
Dose: 200mg OD or BD
